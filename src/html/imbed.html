Imbed LLC.

The goal at Imbed LLC. was to design, develop and improve digital cinema servers by increasing the native resolution, increasing the frame rate of playback, and improve audio by increasing the number and frequency of the audio channels. The project needed to stay within the guidelines of digital cinema standards as defined by SMPTE(Society of Motion Picture and Television Engineers).

XML and MXF Parsing

The first step was to develop code to accept digital cinema files which came in the forms of XML(Extensible Markup Language) and MXF(Material Exchange Format) files. The XML files defined the basic structure of movie playback and the order in which the reels need to be sent to the digital projectors of cinema as well as packaging metadata required by the digital cinema projectors. Once the programs for compilation of the Composition playlists were complete, I moved on to parsing the subtitle files which came in XML format and prepared them to be played alongside video and audio frames by our playback engine.

My next task was to parse and gather information from MXF files which were responsible for the transfer of video and audio data of a movie. These came in the form of encoded packets with corresponding keys designated by the standards of cinema. My job was to parse through these files in the form of a breadth-first search algorithm, logging all keys and nested keys before identifying all necessary packets. Once all the video and audio packets were identified in the tree, I extracted the audio and frames into a folder to be used in digital playback.

Digital Playback Engine

The culmination of my work at Imbed was the digital playback engine. It required the entirety of my programs and parsers in order to function correctly. Using a cyclical buffer, we synchronized the playback of audio and video frames, and if the flag was set, played the subtitles in sync with the rest of the movie. The combination of these 3 projects provided the testing environment for the rest of the embedded applications we would include on the cinema server itself.
